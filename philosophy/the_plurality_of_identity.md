> [!quote] #quote 
In day-to-day life, we tend to think of our identity as being consistent and indivisible, but in reality, we're never exactly the same person from moment to moment. The way you speak to your mother isn't how you speak to your boss. When you're hungry, angry, or in love, the way you act and even think can change quite radically. It's as if you're possessed by a pantheon of spirits, each with their own agenda. Our identities depend as much on context as they do on intrinsic nature. Where we are, who we're with, and what we're doing all affect who we are in the moment. 

I feel this very often, like there isn't a solid entity who I am. Like I am only what I perceive from time to time. The person I see in the mirror never aligns exactly with who I imagine myself to be, whether negatively or positively, and likewise differs in different contexts.

>No self is an island, each exists in a fabric of relations that is more complex and mobile than ever before.
