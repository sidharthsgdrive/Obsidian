> [!info]
> ++Default mode network++: brain regions most active when the mind is at wakeful rest; involved in self-reflection, daydreaming, memory, and imagining the future.

This video resonated with me in a way that YouTube videos rarely do. And this came to me at a time, or rather date (`29/08/25`) when I've been hedonistically consuming content non-stop from the moment I open my eyes in the morning to when I wind down to bed.

Boredom is a tendency for us to not be occupied otherwise cognitively, which switches over our thinking system to use a part of our brain that's called the default mode network. 

In today's world, we've figured out how to eliminate boredom entirely, through the use of mobile phones -- devices that we carry on our person 24/7 in a socially acceptable, non-intrusive manner. Whenever I am bored, in public or otherwise, I find myself whipping out my phone and opening Instagram or some other app for some gratification. What if, instead of all that, I just sat alone with my thoughts?

The video brings up a good point, in that we avoid these [[quiet_moments]] often because they force us to confront uncomfortable thoughts. These thoughts could be of approaching hurdles, past embarrassments, future prospects, or of the very nature of existence and identity.

I think the takeaway here is that a quiet moment is far more beneficial and less wasteful than a cluttered one borne of boredom.
