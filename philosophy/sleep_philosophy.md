### How does sleep feel like?
When I sleep, my eyes are closed, my body is still, and my senses are dampened. In increasing order of acuteness of sense during sleep, they are:
1. Sight: This seems to be the sense most dampened during sleep. The eyes are closed and unfocused, and hence seeing is entirely out of the question.
2. Smell and taste: These are the two senses that are dampened the most, behind sight. During sleep, the smell of my pillow and the taste of my mouth are the only ones I know.
3. Hearing: As I drift off to sleep, hearing is actually sharpened, as vision is lost and the other senses go into overdrive. However, during the actual state of sleep, the ears are somehow physiologically closed (i have to do further research on this), and the sense of sound is thus mostly eliminated.
4. Touch: With stillness, the sensation of touch is also mostly reduced, but is the most felt out of the senses during sleep (like when the sleeping position is uncomfortable and I adjust)
The stillness of sleep seems to be what sets it apart from the awake state. The stillness is also what makes sleep inherently comfortable -- it's a hypnotizing prospect to stay right where you are, in accordance with [[inertia]].
