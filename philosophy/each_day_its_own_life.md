

> [!quote] #quote 
> “Begin at once to live, and count each separate day as a separate life” ~Seneca

I first encountered this quote an year or two ago, back when I started thinking seriously about creating daily routines and [rituals][Rituals (to write)]. I thought this quote greatly inspiring, and I remember taking baths in the morning (a ritual which I have regrettably abandoned over the past year) and thinking to myself, "Here, I am reborn".

I watched a video called [Sleep is just death being shy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-mu780uB7mI) a long time ago. Though I've since forgotten the contents of the video itself, I remember being struck by the succinctness and depth of meaning conveyed by the title. Death is not something I have formed clear views on yet, and I admit it scares me. However, I would like to leave that topic aside for the foreseeable future, as I don't see any productive outcomes coming from worrying about it. Yet, sleep is the trial version of death, and I approach it every night with calm and love. It could be said that sleep is death without commitments, and surely, this view serves to reinforce the idea of living each day as its own life, with the birth of the morning bath and death of sleep.

{Sleep} is very important to me, and serves as the ultimate {timeblock}.
