Over the past few weeks, our class was acquainted with the subject of Engineering Ethics and Sustainable Development. So far, the course instructor has covered the first module, which covered ethics fundamentals, ethics in technology, and the basic concepts of gender studies.
The first lecture was a broad introduction to the fundamentals of ethics. Among the topics covered were the distinction between personal and professional ethics, ingenuity, diligence and responsibility. We were familiarized with the concept of integrity in various domains as well.
In the second lecture, we were introduced to the concept of data, and its collection and management. This was accompanied by a glance at cybersecurity. As part of ethics, we were taught conflict menagement and confidentiality.
For the last topic of the module being Gender Studies, it was conducted as a seminar, where the class was divided into 7 groups, each group having to present a short thesis on gender inequalities and problems in society, their root causes, and potential solutions. As a member of group 1, I laid out the outline for our group's presentation. Through the activity, we learned about gender stereotypes, issues faced by people of various gender in everyday life as well as the workplace, and the importance of gender equity, diversity and justice.
