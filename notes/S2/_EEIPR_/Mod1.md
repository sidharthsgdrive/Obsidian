# Barriers to innovation:
* Lack of resources (time/money/workforce)
* Risk aversion, resistance to change (preferring to stick to what has worked before)
* Lack of diversity (homogeneity)
* Bureaucracy (processes that slow or prevent innovation)
* Lack of employee recognition
***
# Types of Innovation Frameworks
* **Incremental Framework**:  small, continuous improvements to existing offerings, gradually evolving the product. Effective in mature markets with less disruption.
* **Disruptive Framework**: introducing products and services that disrupt the market or even create entirely new markets. Works by identifying niches where groundbreaking solutions can be offered. High risk, high reward.
* **Open Framework**: Leverages external ideas and resources by collaborating with partners such as customers, startups, academic institutions. Allows for a diverse array of expertise and ideas to be incorporated into the product.
* **Design Thinking Framework**: User centric approach centered around empathy, ideation and prototyping. 
***
# Patents
* A legal right that protects an invention for the set period of time.
* It is a territorial right that grants the inventor monopoly in the registered country
* Grants the applicant the sole right to manufacture and sell the product
* Applicable to a wide range of inventions
* To apply, the inventor must disclose technical information about the invention to the public, in a patent application.
***
# Copyrights
* It is a legal right that gives the creator of an IP the exclusive right to reproduce or distribute that work. 
* Applicable to literary and artistic works.
***
# Trademarks
* A form of IP that consists of an identifying mark that distinguishes the product as originating from a particular company.
* Could be a word, phrase, symbol, design, scent, colour.
* Primary function is to prevent consumers from confusing the source of a product with other goods.
* Trademark laws allow owners to enforce against infringement.
*** 
# Trade secrets
* Confidential information that gives a business an edge over the competition.
* Could be a formula, recipe, design, manufacturing technique, or protocol
* To qualify as a trade secret, the information must be commercially valuable, known to a limited group, and be kept secret
***
# IP protection strategies
* Patent application
* Confidentiality agreements, NDAs
* Security measures to prevent against unauthorized access, data breaches, etc
* IP audits
* IP strategy to be reviewed regularly and evolved along with the company
* IP awareness among employees and stakeholders
