# **Components of a Business Plan**
1. Summary/Description
2. Business Plan Framework
3. Market Analysis
4. Description
5. Marketing & Sales Strategy
6. Operations Plan
7. Financial Projections
8. Risk Management, risk-return-exit
***
# **Market Analysis**

Steps: 
1. Research your industry
2. Investigate the competitive landscape
3. Identify market gaps
4. Define target market
5. Identify barriers to entry
6. Create sales forecast
***
# **Product/Service Description**

Steps:
1. Identify target customers
2. Relate to your audience's interests and needs
3. Format accordingly
4. Be specific and focus on benefits
5. Incorporate quantifiable evidence 
6. Use storytelling to engage your customer's imaginations
***
# **Marketing & Sales Strategy**

Steps:
1. Identify goals
2. Create customer profiles
3. Develop a message
4. Define your budget
5. Select your communication channels
6. Track metrics
***
# **Financial Projections**

# Income Statement
Also known as P&L statement. Demonstrates profitability by listing the company's net income growth or loss over a specified period of time. Includes
1. Revenue
2. Expenses
3. Net Income = Revenue - Expenses
Using a P&L, we can identify metrics such as profitability.
# Balance Sheet
Demonstrates a snapshot of a business' net worth at a particular moment in time, showing what the company owns and owes. Financial data is represented through:
1. Assets (tangible objects of value)
2. Liabilities (debts)
3. Equity = Assets - Liabilities
Must be balanced (Assets = Liabilities + Equity)
# Cash Flow Projection
Shows how much money is flowing in and out of the business, and cash on hand at a specific time. Used by lenders and investors to determine credit risk. Represented through
1. Cash revenues (sales over a given time)
2. Cash disbursements (expenditures over a given time)
3. Reconciliation of revenues to disbursements = Revenues - Disbursements

# **Components of a Prototype Development Plan**
1. Types of prototyping
2. Technical specifications
3. Development approach
4. Development timeline
5. Resource allocation
6. Testing & QA
7. Iterative development, feedback loop
8. Documentation & Version Control
***
# **Types of prototyping models**
* Rapid throwaway (each time made from scratch)
* Evolutionary (incrementally refined based on feedback)
* Incremental (product broken into pieces, developed individually, integrated; time saved due to simultaneous prototyping and testing of components)
* Extreme (mostly for webdev; in first phase, static prototype; in seocnd phase, functional screens)
***
# **Waterfall Approach**
A software development approach consisting of six phases:
1. Gather requirements
2. Design (architecture, interface, components)
3. Development
4. Testing
5. Deployment
6. Maintenance
***
# **Development Timeline**
Analogous to development approach.
1. Generating concepts
2. Research and Analyze
3. Detailed Blueprinting
4. Preliminary model creation
5. Beta release, refinement
6. Product launch

# **Testing vs QA**

| **Testing**                                                         | **Quality Assurance**                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Involves finding and documenting defects                            | Involves ensuring that developed product meets customer's requirements and is of desired quality |
| Involves unit testing, functional testing, integration testing, etc | Involves root cause analysis, process audits, defect management, etc.                            |
| Takes more cost, time and effort                                    | Takes less cost, time and effort                                                                 |
***
# **Iterative Development**
A software development approach that breaks the process into smaller, more manageable chunks called *iterations* or *sprints*. Each iteration includes planning, design, development, and testing.
***
# **Feedback Loops**
A system where some or all of the output is used as input for future operations. In software development, the feedback loop is formed when the output of one sprint is used to inform the next sprint.

* **Positive feedback loops**: Processes where the output of the system reinforces the system's behavior, i.e. the result feeds back in such a way that makes the system more likely to repeat the process in the future. E.G: The success of a software product leads to more resources being allocated to its development, which leads to further improvement and more success
* **Negative feedback loops**: Processes where the output of the system dampens the system's behavior. In other words, it is a self-regulating mechanism that maintains stability of the system. E.G: A software product encounters a bug, leading to actions fixing it, preventing future occurences.
