It seems a value proposition differs from a unique selling proposition, which tries to convince the market that our product stands out from the competitors. I had earlier assumed that they were the same thing. However, now I understand that a value proposition involves identifying and communicating the value/benefits provided by the product, and relating those benefits to problems faced by the consumer.

Market validation involves presenting our product to the market to see if it is worth pursuing and understand the target market's needs and dynamics (product-market fit)
The steps of market validation are: 
1. Assess business goals
2. Create test questions to ask potential customers
3. Find participants to interview
4. Conduct surveys or interviews
5. Analyze the data
6. Proceed with A/B testing, alpha and beta testing
The goal is to use methods like surveys, interviews, focus groups, prototyping, and minimum viable product to get feedback and iterate based on the feedback to better suit the product to the market.
