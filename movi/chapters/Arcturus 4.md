He knelt in the Great Hall of the Byssine palace, seat of the erstwhile High Sith emperors for centuries.
"What tidings do you bring, Arcturus?" came the voice from above. It was not a booming voice, but it rang clear across the empty court all the same, echoing off the thick marble walls.
"News of victory, my Lord," Arcturus said sofly, his eyes still on the floor, but aware of the probing gaze of his master, piercing through his skull, piercing through his mind. Sometimes he wondered Exodus even bothered talking to him; so accomplished was he at the mental side of the Force.
He produced his spear, gold stained red, from his back-strap, and laid it in his hands, palms open to the ceiling -- the gift of a servant who has done his duty. "They're dead. Every one of them on Megalon."
There was no emotional reaction from Exodus. "Good," he said. "They did not pose a challenge to you?"
"No," said Arcturus. Unconsciously, his hand went to the crook of his elbow. "And that?"  his master questioned, picking up on the movement.
"Their leader," Arcturus muttered. "Caught me by surprise."
There was a long silence, as Exodus reached into his apprentice's mind. He took a deep breath, and climbed down from the High Seat, to accept the Golden Spear. "And the Movi?" 
Arcturus looked up, startled. He hadn't meant to give that away. He measured his words carefully before speaking. "There were only two, and a mercenary. I spared them."
"And your getaway was uninterrupted?"
"....No."
"It was the one you seek, was it not?"
The older man reached down to hold Arcturus' shoulders, and allowed him to rise.
"It wasn't the fight I was looking for," said Arcturus, now in frustration. "He had the Iron Fleet behind him."
"Am I right in saying you crave a more direct encounter?" said Exodus, looking into his eyes.
"...."
"In any case, you have acquitted yourself well, Arcturus," he said finally. "I will hold you back no longer."
Arcturus looked up in gratitude.
