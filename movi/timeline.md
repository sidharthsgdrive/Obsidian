+ 2325 BBK
+ Meluhan Civil War
+ For near two hundred years, the Dwarkans were intermittently persecuted by their Asura Overlords. They continued their idolatrous practices in secret, in their ancestral tunnels and caves. A volatile political landscape set the stage for a bloody nine-year-long civil war, where a Dwarkan woman named Kyralia emerged victorious, vowing to reshape the Empire known till now as Meluha.

+ 2316 BBK
+ Ascension
+ Upon ascending the throne, the Dwarkans wasted no time in consolidating power in the nearby star systems. At the Council of Qimir, five systems swore allegiance to Kyralia, and an Imperial Constitution was drafted, with provisions for expansion under a federal structure that could eventually rule the whole Galaxy.
Kyralia's rule spread fast, growing to encompass nearly the whole Outer Rim, and much of the Mid Rim. Many Sith worlds took their chance in the chaos to declare independence. The Inner Core worlds developed into a Republic governed by a handful of Great Houses, whereas the Sith Heartlands in the Outer Rim formed kingdoms constantly at war with each other and with their neighbours, being the Presidency, Hutt worlds, and Kyralia. Why did the Asura let their Presidency fall from the position of Overlord?

+ 2300 BBK
+ Age of Gods
+ For nearly two thousand years, there was a stable multipolar galaxy, with the Presidency, Kingdoms and Empire balancing each other. In this time, there was significant galactic trade, causing many systems to lose their self-sufficiency in favour of specialized economy. However, this proved beneficial for most worlds, with unprecedented growth in infrastructure, population and welfare. 
Behind the scenes, the Asura worked tirelessly to prevent technological progress too rapid for them to control. This meant that no faction could dominate the Galaxy until the End Times -- and thus their Great Game was played.
