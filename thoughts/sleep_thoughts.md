***
`20/07/25` To add to this, my practice of turning the fan off and getting back in bed doesn't help either -- at least in the rainy season, the cold air from outside persists, and the body is slow to warm up. In conclusion, I believe it is better to brace myself and face the cold once and for all, as the days when I am able to wake up 
1. on time,
2. warm,
3. with less than 9 hours of sleep,
4. and rested 
are very few, perhaps **one in twenty**. Yes, sleep quality is important, but which should I prioritize -- the feeling of getting out of bed warm, or being on time? The answer seems easy to me when I consider that getting out of bed is bound to be unsatisfying on some level anyway, so I might as well spring out of bed without bothering to optimize my comfort at that moment.
