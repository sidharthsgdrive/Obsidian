(Dates I finished reading)

`09/08/25` Sunrise at the Reaping -- Suzanne Collins
A surprisingly engrossing return to a franchise I thought to have been milked dry, with plenty of references to the other spin-off book and the main series, while retaining its identity. The character setups and first two parts were good, while the actual game was somewhat muddling and all over the place, though I suppose that is part of the point. The ending and prologue were especially emotional, and made me cry.
***
`05/09/25` Foundation -- Isaac Asimov
A truly epic read. I can see why this inspired works like Star Wars. This book tickled the part of my brain that craves for giant empires and massive movements. Each arc felt cohesive and important to the plot. Can't wait for the inevitable clash the Foundation will have with the Imperial Remnants.
***
`25/09/25` Foundation and Empire -- Isaac Asimov
A continuation to the series that went above and beyond its predecessor in terms of scale and value. The theme of the Imperial Remnants remained intact well into the final act, when it seemed like it was all lost. Bel Riose's defeat and the conclusion of the Siwenna arc was satisfying. 
A fantastic arc was the siege of Haven and occupation of the Foundaiton by the Mule. It evoked some poignant emotions, and ended in a fantastic anticlimax in Han Pritcher's POV.
The Neotrantor and Trantor arc was also beautifully written; the weight of the Sack and desolation of the core worlds apparent and inspiring thought.
The final reveal of the Mule was as shocking as it was satisfying, explaining all the plotholes in the second half of the book at a stroke.
I love how these books make it very clear what's going to be the title of the sequel. In this case, it's clearly Second Foundation.
***
`12/10/25` It's been a few days since I finished reading Second Foundation. I must admit, the first half of the book was pretty mid. I think the takedown of the Mule could have been done a bit better. However, the second half was fairly engaging, and built up the Second Foundation very well.
